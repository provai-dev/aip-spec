# Authorship And Contributors

Standards work requires clear credit without blurring authorship, editorship, and review roles.

## Categories Of Contribution

### Document Authors Or Editors

Reserved for the people publicly accountable for the draft's content and editorial control.

Changes to authorship or editor lists should be deliberate and maintainer-approved.

### Contributors

People who made substantial issue analysis, proposal drafting, review, validation, or text contributions but are not listed as document authors or editors.

Contributors should be credited in issue history, pull requests, acknowledgments, or a future contributor list if one is maintained.

### Reviewers

People who provided meaningful review, compatibility analysis, or subject-matter guidance.

Reviewer credit should be preserved in issue and PR history where practical.

## Responsibility

Credit and decision-making responsibility remain with the humans who submit, review, and approve contributions.

## Commit And PR Attribution

- Keep the public record tied to the humans responsible for submission and approval.
- Use issue and PR descriptions to preserve rationale and acknowledgments.
- Avoid burying substantive authorship changes inside unrelated PRs.

## Acknowledgment Guidance

Use acknowledgments for:

- significant proposal input
- careful issue research
- implementation experience that materially informed the draft
- review that changed the final outcome

Do not use acknowledgments to imply endorsement of text someone did not approve.
